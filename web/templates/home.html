What is Ransomware?

Ransomware is a type of malicious software designed to block access to a computer system or data until a ransom is paid. It typically encrypts the victim's data, making it inaccessible, and displays a message demanding payment in exchange for the decryption key.

How Does Ransomware Work?

Ransomware often spreads through phishing emails, malicious websites, or infected software downloads. Once it infects a system, it begins encrypting files and displays a ransom note, warning the user to pay a specific amount—usually in cryptocurrency—to regain access to their data.

Types of Ransomware

  • Crypto Ransomware: Encrypts files and demands a ransom for the decryption key.
  • Locker Ransomware: Locks the user out of their device entirely, rendering it unusable.
  • Scareware: Pretends to be a legitimate warning but demands payment to fix non-existent problems.

How to Protect Yourself?

Protecting against ransomware requires proactive measures, including:

  • Regularly backing up important data and storing it offline or in the cloud.
  • Keeping your software and operating system up-to-date.
  • Avoiding clicking on suspicious links or downloading attachments from unknown sources.
  • Installing and maintaining reliable antivirus and anti-malware software.

What to Do if Infected?

If your system is infected with ransomware:

  • Do not pay the ransom—it doesn’t guarantee data recovery and encourages attackers.
  • Disconnect your device from the network to prevent further spread.
  • Seek help from cybersecurity professionals or law enforcement.
  • Restore your data from a backup, if available.
